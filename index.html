The Discovery

DNA Double Helix Structure

Nobel Prize awarded to Watson, Crick, and Wilkins in 1962

The Woman Behind It

Rosalind Franklin

Her X-ray diffraction photographs, particularly Photo 51, were foundational to understanding DNA's structure. Her work was shown to Watson and Crick without her permission.

Nuclear Fission

Nobel Prize awarded to Otto Hahn in 1944

The Woman Behind It

Lise Meitner

Co-discovered and explained nuclear fission with Hahn, but was excluded from the Nobel Prize despite her crucial theoretical contributions.

The Discovery

Radio Pulsars

Nobel Prize awarded to Antony Hewish and Martin Ryle in 1974

The Woman Behind It

Jocelyn Bell Burnell

As a graduate student, she made the initial observation and discovery of the first radio pulsars, but was excluded from the Nobel Prize.

Woman of the Day

🧬

Rosalind Franklin

1920 - 1958

British biophysicist whose X-ray diffraction work provided critical evidence for understanding the structure of DNA. Her precise measurements and photographs, particularly "Photo 51," revealed the helical structure that would lead to one of the greatest scientific discoveries of the 20th century. Despite her foundational contributions, she was largely uncredited during her lifetime.

"Science and everyday life cannot and should not be separated."
